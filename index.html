Series 1: Remote Meeting Dynamics (Starting, Ending, and Tech Issues)

Starting the Meeting

"__**Shall we begin?**__, everyone? Can everyone hear me clearly?"

Shall we begin?

Tip:

Use this phrase to formally start meetings or presentations, establishing control.

Quick Exit

"__**I got to go now**__, I have another call starting now, talk to you later."

I got to go now

Tip:

Casual yet direct way to end a conversation due to another obligation. (Use "I must conclude" for formal settings.)

Audio Quality Check

"If you can't hear me well, just say __**Please speak up!**__. Can you hear me?"

Please speak up!

Tip:

Politely ask participants to speak louder if you can't hear them.

Poor Connection

"You're __**breaking up**__, your audio keeps cutting out."

breaking up

Tip:

Describe poor audio quality during calls where the voice is intermittent.

Handling Distractions

"Sorry to interrupt, but there's too much __**background noise**__ in the background."

background noise

Tip:

Mention extraneous sounds that may disrupt meetings and request muting.

Asking for Repetition

"__**Sorry, I beg your pardon**__, could you repeat that? I didn‚Äôt catch the last part."

Sorry, I beg your pardon

Tip:

Politely ask for repetition when you didn't fully understand the speaker.

Visual Delays 1

"My video keeps freezing, it looks like __**The video is buffering**__. What should I do?"

The video is buffering

Tip:

This indicates internet speed is slow, causing media delays.

Visual Delays 2

"Sorry, your connection is unstable, __**Your video is lagging**__ so we don't miss anything."

Your video is lagging

Tip:

This phrase indicates delayed or choppy video, impacting the meeting flow.

Late Arrival Reason

"The __**other meeting overran**__ made me miss the start of this call."

other meeting overran

Tip:

Explain that a prior meeting took longer than its scheduled time.

Series 2: Office and Administrative Reporting

Defining Hierarchy

"In this project, I __**report to**__ the marketing manager."

report to

Tip:

Describes who your direct supervisor or manager is in a project or company structure.

Confirming Participation

"I __**attended**__ a client meeting yesterday regarding the contract renewal."

attended

Tip:

Use this to report your formal participation in meetings or events.

Resource Status

"We can't use that conference room now because __**The meeting room is busy**__."

The meeting room is busy

Tip:

Explain why a room or resource is currently unavailable.

Commuting Delay

"I‚Äôm __**stuck in traffic**__; I might be a few minutes late for the meeting."

stuck in traffic

Tip:

Common and acceptable excuse for delays when commuting.

Temporary Absence

"I'm __**away from my desk**__ at the moment, but you can leave a message."

away from my desk

Tip:

Let others know you're temporarily unavailable for calls or quick checks.

Leave Request

"I have to apply for __**sick leave**__ due to illness."

sick leave

Tip:

Official period of absence from work for health reasons.

Document Delivery

"The __**courier**__ just delivered the contract to my office."

courier

Tip:

The person or service responsible for delivering important packages.

Onsite Health Service

"The __**outpatient department**__ handles employee health and safety consultations onsite."

outpatient department

Tip:

Medical area where employees receive health services without admission.

Series 3: Proactive Professional Language (Action, Finance, General)

Step-by-Step Request

"Could you __**Please walk me through**__, please? I need to understand the process better."

Please walk me through

Tip:

Request someone to explain a process or document step-by-step.

Polite Interruption

"Before we continue, I have __**just a quick question**__. Can you clarify the deadlines?"

just a quick question

Tip:

Use it to interrupt politely with a brief inquiry, minimizing disruption.

Contextual Reminder

"__**Bear in mind**__, the client prefers written communication via email."

Bear in mind

Tip:

Use this to remind others of important facts or instructions before they act.

Encouraging Follow-up

"Could you __**chase the team**__, and make sure everyone completes their parts on time?"

chase the team

Tip:

Encourage following up with colleagues to meet deadlines and ensure completion.

Encouragement

"__**Seize the opportunity**__! Don‚Äôt let this chance slip away."

Tip:

Encourage acting quickly and decisively to benefit from a situation.

Allocated Money

"The __**budget**__ for this quarter‚Äôs marketing campaign has been reduced."

Tip:

Refers to the allocated money for a specific project or department.

Quality Assurance

"We need to __**run tests**__ before the product launch next week."

Tip:

Conduct quality or functional tests pre-launch to identify issues.

Resistance

"We experienced some __**pushback**__ from the client regarding the contract terms."

pushback

Tip:

Resistance or objections encountered during negotiations or project implementation.
